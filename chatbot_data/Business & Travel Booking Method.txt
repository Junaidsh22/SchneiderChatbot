
Below are the list of rules that are laid out and should be followed if booking expenses or travel.

Step 1: CONFIRM BUSINESS NEED

•	Identify the purpose of the trip (e.g. customer visit, internal meeting, training).
•	Ensure the trip is necessary and aligned with business goals.
•	Check your BFO diary (if applicable) to confirm appointments.
________________________________________

Step 2: EVALUATE TRAVEL OPTIONS

Company Car	If trip is under 150 miles or 3 hours. Required if you have a company car.
Rail Travel	Book via SE Trainline Portal (Standard Class only).
Car Hire	Book via SE Hertz Portal (Group CCMR cars like Vauxhall Astra).
Taxi	Only if short distance and unavoidable.

CONSIDER:
•	Cost
•	Travel time
•	Convenience
•	Environmental impact
•	Policy compliance
________________________________________

Step 3: REQUEST LINE MANAGER APPROVAL

Send a formal request to your Line Manager including:
•	Trip purpose
•	Preferred travel method
•	Estimated costs
•	Any overnight stay requirements

Wait for explicit approval before booking.
________________________________________

Step 4: BOOK VIA APPROVED TRAVEL PORTALS

•	Rail Travel: Book via SE Trainline Portal.
•	Car Hire: Book via SE Hertz Portal.
•	Company Car: Use if applicable and log mileage.
•	Taxi: Minimise use; collect VAT receipt.

 Always use official portals unless you have written approval to use alternatives.
________________________________________

Step 5: BOOKING OVERNIGHT ACCOMADATION

•	Confirm if a hotel stay is needed.
•	Book within pricing limits: 
o	UK: £90/night
o	London: £160/night
•	If staying with friends/relatives, claim £50/night (taxable).
•	Use Concur for hotel bookings and expense tracking.
________________________________________

Step 6: PLAN AHEAD WITH SUBSTINENCE & DAILY ALLOWANCES 

Type of Expense	Amount	Conditions
Breakfast	£10	Only if staying overnight
Dinner	£30	For extended business trips (includes drinks & VAT)
Off-site Travel	£4.15/day	If on the road for 5+ hours
Lunch (UK)	Not claimable	Only claimable when abroad

________________________________________

Step 7: ADDITIONAL PASSENGER ALLOWANCE

•	If you take another SE employee in your company car: 
o	Claim £0.10 per mile per passenger.
o	Submit via the expense system.
________________________________________

Step 8: PREPARE FOR EXTRA/OTHER EXPENSES
Use your Corporate AMEX Card for all business expenses (if possible)

EXAMPLE OF CLAIMABLE EXPENSES:
•	Parking, tolls, congestion charges
•	Hotel internet
•	Eye tests & work-related glasses (VDU certified)
•	Batteries & consumables
•	Flight luggage fees
•	Emergency project purchases (VP pre-approval required)
•	Foreign exchange (with invoice)
•	Laundry (if away >4 nights)
•	One newspaper/day (if staying overnight)
•	Mileage (via HMRC AFR portal)
•	£0.10/mile per SE passenger in your car

EXAMPLE OF NON CLAIMABLE EXPENSES:
•	Stationery (collect from office)
•	Traffic fines
•	In-flight movies/refreshments (unless meals not included)
•	Unapproved material purchases
•	Hotel room movies
•	Personal items (toiletries, clothing)
•	Car washes/screen wash (except pool cars)
•	Car repairs (organised via LeasePlan)
•	Hairdressing, gifts
•	Workwear/PPE (must be sourced via approved vendor)
•	Luggage/briefcases
•	Other non-policy expenses
________________________________________

Step 9: SUBMIT EXPENSES ON TIME AND ACCORDINGLY 

•	Submit via Concur: 
o	Monthly (for regular claimants)
o	Every 2 months (for infrequent claimants)
•	Include: 
o	Valid VAT receipts
o	Detailed descriptions
o	Mileage logs (if applicable)
________________________________________

Step 10: POST TRIP CHECKLIST 

•	Submit all expenses promptly.
•	Update BFO diary (Sales Engineers only).
•	Ensure all data and documents from the trip are stored securely.
