Q: What is the first step when booking business travel or expenses?
A: Confirm the business need by identifying the purpose of the trip, ensuring it is necessary, and checking your BFO diary if applicable.

Q: How should I choose a travel option?
A: Evaluate cost, travel time, convenience, environmental impact, and policy compliance. Use:
• Company car for trips under 150 miles or 3 hours (mandatory if you have one).
• Rail via the SE Trainline Portal (Standard Class).
• Car hire via the SE Hertz Portal.
• Taxis only when unavoidable.

Q: Do I need approval before booking travel?
A: Yes. Send your Line Manager the trip purpose, travel method, estimated costs, and any hotel needs. Wait for explicit approval before booking.

Q: How do I book travel once approved?
A: Always use approved portals: SE Trainline for rail, SE Hertz for hire cars, and log mileage when using a company car. Minimise taxi use and keep VAT receipts.

Q: How do I book overnight accommodation?
A: Confirm if a hotel stay is required. Stay within limits (£90 UK, £160 London). Claim £50/night (taxable) when staying with friends/relatives. Use Concur for hotel bookings and expenses.

Q: What subsistence and daily allowances can I claim?
A:
• Breakfast: £10 (only with overnight stay)
• Dinner: £30 (extended trips, includes drinks & VAT)
• Off-site allowance: £4.15/day (on the road 5+ hours)
• Lunch in the UK: not claimable

Q: Can I claim for taking another employee in my company car?
A: Yes. Claim £0.10 per mile per SE passenger.

Q: What expenses are claimable?
A: Examples include parking, tolls, congestion charges, hotel internet, VDU eye tests/glasses, consumables, flight luggage, approved emergency purchases, foreign exchange fees, laundry if away >4 nights, one newspaper/day during overnight stays, mileage, and passenger allowance.

Q: What expenses are not claimable?
A: Examples include stationery, traffic fines, in-flight entertainment, unapproved purchases, hotel movies, personal items, car washes (except pool cars), car repairs, hairdressing, gifts, PPE/workwear from non-approved sources, luggage, and other non-policy items.

Q: When should I submit expenses?
A: Submit via Concur monthly (regular) or every two months (infrequent), including VAT receipts, descriptions, and mileage logs.

Q: What should I do after the trip?
A: Submit all expenses, update the BFO diary if required, and store all documents and data securely.
