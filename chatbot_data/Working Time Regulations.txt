Q: What is the purpose of Schneider Electric’s Working Time policies?
A: They promote fairness, wellbeing, and productivity, ensuring employees receive proper rest and managers lead with care and awareness of working time standards.

Q: What counts as working time?
A:
• Performing job duties (on-site or remote)
• Client-facing work
• Mandatory training
• Required on-call time at a specified location
• Work-related travel to non-office locations (e.g., conferences, client sites)

Q: What does NOT count as working time?
A:
• Commuting to your normal workplace
• Personal time or voluntary breaks
• Travel to your regular office location

Q: What are the required rest and break rules?
A:
• Minimum 20-minute uninterrupted break if working more than 6 hours
• At least 11 hours of uninterrupted rest in each 24-hour period
• 24 hours rest per week, or 48 hours rest over two weeks

Q: What are the night work rules?
A:
• Night work should not exceed 8 hours per night on average over 17 weeks
• Night workers are employees who regularly work at least 3 hours between 9 PM and 6 AM

Q: What additional rules apply to young workers under 19?
A: They have enhanced rest protections. Managers, employees, and HR must review the relevant policies to ensure compliance.

Q: What are the annual leave entitlements?
A:
• 28 days paid leave per year (including bank holidays)
• Leave year runs from 1 January to 31 December
• Leave must be taken within the year
• Limited carry-over allowed only until 31 March
• Annual leave supports work-life balance and should be planned responsibly
