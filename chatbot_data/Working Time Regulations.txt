Working Time Regulations (Training)

OVERVIEW:
All employees are expected to work in alignment with Schneider Electric’s established HR policies relating to working time. 
These policies are designed to promote fairness, wellbeing, and overall productivity. Rest and recovery are recognised as essential components of delivering high-quality work, and managers are expected to lead with care, respect, and awareness of these standards.

DEFINITION OF WORKING TIME:
Time spent performing job duties (on-site or remotely)
Time spent engaged in client work
Time spent undertaking mandatory training
Time spent on-call while present at a required location
Travel required for non-office work activities (e.g., conferences, client sites)
(WHAT IT DOES NOT INCLUDE):
Commuting to and from your regular workplace
Personal time or breaks not required for work
Travel to your normal office location


REST/BREAK POLICY:
Employees must take a minimum 20-minute uninterrupted break if their working day exceeds 6 hours.
A minimum of 11 hours of uninterrupted rest must be provided within each 24-hour period.
24 hours of rest per week, or 48 hours of rest every two weeks.

NIGHT WORK:
Should not exceed 8 hours of work per night, averaged over a 17-week period.
Are defined as employees who work at least 3 hours between 9:00 PM and 6:00 AM, consistently (not occasionally).

YOUNG WORKERS (UNDER 19):
Young workers are entitled to enhanced rest periods and additional protections.
Employees, managers, or HR representatives should review the relevant policies to ensure compliance.

INFORMATION RELATED TO LEAVE/ ANNUAL HOLIDAYS:
28 days of paid leave per calendar year, including bank holidays
Holiday year runs: 1 January – 31 December
Leave must be taken in-year
Any unused leave may be carried over only up to 31 March, in line with policy
Annual leave is provided to support work-life balance and must be planned responsibly.
